# Source Priority

Use fresh browsing for this skill. The report is supposed to reflect the latest state of the asset.

## Priority Order

1. Official asset sources:
   docs, blog, roadmap, governance forum, GitHub, foundation updates, official X accounts; for stocks also include IR pages, earnings releases, shareholder letters, 10-K/10-Q/20-F/8-K, investor day materials, and official earnings transcripts when available
2. Primary market and on-chain sources:
   token pages, explorers, staking dashboards, treasury dashboards, protocol analytics; for stocks also include quote pages, valuation dashboards, consensus summaries, ownership data, and sector market-data sources
3. High-quality secondary research:
   reputable exchanges, research desks, established analytics firms, major industry publications
4. Community and social discussion:
   direct X posts first, then reliable summaries or thread roundups if direct access is limited
5. Corporate filing and earnings sources when relevant:
   SEC or exchange filings, annual reports, quarterly reports, earnings decks, call transcripts, shareholder letters, and investor presentations
6. Unlock and vesting sources when relevant:
   official tokenomics pages, vesting schedules, governance posts, treasury disclosures, vesting contracts, then reputable unlock dashboards such as Tokenomist, Token Unlocks, or CryptoRank as secondary fallback
7. Node and validator sources when relevant:
   official validator pages, explorers, staking dashboards, telemetry sites, client dashboards, or reputable infrastructure trackers

## Writing Rules

- Quote or paraphrase only what you can source.
- Use exact timestamps for roadmap changes, token unlocks, node snapshots, earnings releases, guidance changes, and major announcements.
- For unlocks, distinguish between cliff, linear, programmatic, and already vested but still controlled supply instead of lumping all supply pressure together.
- When using a secondary unlock dashboard, say so explicitly and prefer cross-checking it against official tokenomics or governance material.
- For stocks, distinguish GAAP and non-GAAP figures, TTM and forward numbers, basic and diluted share count, and recurring performance versus one-off accounting noise.
- On repeat coverage, compare fresh findings against the newest prior report and prioritize the net-new facts first.
- When inferring sentiment from X, identify who is speaking:
  team, builders, long-only bulls, traders, skeptics, competitors, or researchers.
- Distinguish between shipped milestones and promised milestones.
- Distinguish between token value capture and ecosystem growth. A chain can grow while the token captures little value.
- Distinguish between AI utility and AI narrative. Many assets have one without the other.
- For node data, distinguish validators, full nodes, archive nodes, RPC nodes, light clients, and observers whenever the source makes that distinction. Do not present one as another.
- For stocks, prefer the company's own filings and IR materials over summary commentary when there is a conflict.

## Price Forecast Rules

- Present `bear`, `base`, and `bull` cases.
- Default to a next-12-month horizon unless the user explicitly asks for a specific calendar year.
- State the assumption set behind each case.
- Mention what would break the thesis.
- Avoid precise false precision; approximate ranges are better than overly specific single targets.
- Do not present the forecast as personal financial advice.

## Macro and Policy Rules

- Tie macro views to transmission channels such as liquidity, real rates, dollar strength, recession risk, and sector rotation.
- Tie policy views to specific jurisdictions or rulemaking bodies when possible.
- Separate broad crypto policy from token-specific policy risk.
- For stocks, tie policy views to the concrete agencies, jurisdictions, tariffs, export controls, subsidy regimes, or accounting rules that affect the company.
- Do not assume favorable regulation; spell out both upside and downside policy paths.

## Infrastructure Detail Rules

- For explorer links, prefer the official explorer first, then the most widely used community explorer.
- For stocks, prefer the investor-relations page first, then the primary filing database or exchange disclosure page.
- For smart contract language or VM details, prefer official documentation over third-party tutorials.
- Distinguish between token standard languages, smart contract languages, app-layer languages, and node/client languages.
