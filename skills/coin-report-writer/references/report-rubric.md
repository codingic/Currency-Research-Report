# Report Rubric

Follow this order unless the user explicitly asks for a different structure.

## Required Structure

If the asset already has an earlier report in the workspace, keep the full structure below but weight each section toward incremental changes since the previous report rather than re-explaining stable background at the same depth.

1. `与上次相比的关键变化`
   If a prior report exists, summarize the most important deltas first. If not, state that this is first coverage and list the newest drivers that matter now.
2. `关键指标快照`
   Include the most decision-useful metrics for this asset and state clearly when metrics are unavailable or unreliable.
3. `解锁与供给压力快照`
   Include the most recent material unlock, the next known unlock, the next 30/90/365 day unlock pressure when available, recipient buckets, and whether the unlock is cliff-based, linear, programmatic, or already largely absorbed. If the token is largely fully circulating or reliable unlock data is unavailable, say so explicitly.
4. `节点与验证者概况`
   For chain or network assets, state total node count and/or validator count, specify what class of nodes the figure refers to, note concentration or distribution where possible, and explain why it matters for decentralization, liveness, or censorship resistance. If node data is unavailable or not applicable, say so explicitly.
5. `股票信息与核心指标`
   For stock or listed-company assets, include ticker, exchange, core business, market cap, enterprise value, revenue and growth, gross margin, operating margin, EPS, free cash flow, net cash or debt, diluted share count, valuation multiples, and any material SBC, buyback, or convertible context. If the asset is not a stock, say `不适用`.
6. `赛道定位`
   Name the primary sector and optional secondary sector. Explain briefly why this framework is the right one for this asset.
7. `核心投资逻辑`
   State the shortest serious bull case and the main path by which the asset creates or accumulates value.
8. `反方观点与证伪条件`
   State the strongest skeptical case and the concrete data or events that would weaken the thesis.
9. `市场可能忽略的变量`
   Highlight the variables that may be underpriced, delayed, controversial, or hard to model.
10. `对标项目比较`
   Use [comparables.md](comparables.md). Compare against 2-4 close peers in the same sector or same project category, and explain relative strengths, weaknesses, and valuation context.
11. `Executive Summary`
   Cover what the asset is, what changed recently, what is newly important since the last report if one exists, the current investment debate, and your top-line conclusion in 4-8 sentences.
12. `历史大事件与影响意义`
   Pick the 3-8 historical events that most changed the asset's trajectory. Cover what happened, the direct impact at that time, the longer-term significance, and whether the event still affects the current thesis. For stocks, prioritize listing, strategic pivots, major product cycles, acquisitions, management changes, margin inflections, regulatory cases, or capital-structure turning points.
13. `1. Recent Ecosystem Progress`
   Include launches, integrations, funding, governance changes, adoption metrics, developer activity, and notable ecosystem partnerships. For stocks, reinterpret this as key business progress, product launches, customers, orders, guidance changes, capex developments, or important industry shifts.
14. `2. Recent X Discussion`
   Summarize what the official account, founders, core contributors, analysts, and skeptics are discussing. Mention both bullish and bearish threads. For stocks, include management commentary, analyst debate, major bull and bear threads, and customer or channel discussion when relevant.
15. `3. Tokenomics`
   For crypto, cover supply, emissions, recent unlocks, next material unlock windows, staking, burn mechanics, treasury, fee flows, and where dilution or reflexivity may matter. For stocks, reinterpret this section as share count, dilution or SBC, buybacks, convertibles, debt or cash, and capital-structure pressure.
16. `4. Outlook Analysis`
   Assess product-market fit, ecosystem momentum, execution quality, moat, competitors, regulatory exposure, and narrative strength.
17. `5. Value Capture Analysis`
   Explain how users, developers, validators, stakers, sequencers, or treasury mechanisms convert activity into token demand or cash-like flows. For stocks, explain how revenue becomes gross profit, operating income, EPS, free cash flow, and shareholder return per share.
18. `6. Next 12-Month Catalysts and Important Events`
   List the concrete events, milestones, unlocks, upgrades, governance votes, or macro dependencies that could matter over the next 12 months. For stocks, also include earnings dates, product cycles, capex inflections, guidance resets, regulatory decisions, and capital-allocation events. If the user explicitly asks for a calendar year such as 2026, adapt the window to that year.
19. `7. Next 12-Month Price Forecast`
   Use bear/base/bull scenarios with assumptions, invalidation conditions, and approximate ranges for the next 12 months. For stocks, tie the scenarios to revenue growth, margins, EPS or free cash flow, and valuation multiple assumptions. Keep it explicitly non-certain.
20. `8. Multi-angle Analysis`
   Use [angle-framework.md](angle-framework.md). Cover at least 8 relevant angles when possible, including technology, product/demand, users/adoption, token supply-demand, value capture, competition, governance/team, security, liquidity/market structure, community/narrative, macro sensitivity, policy risk, AI relevance, ecosystem dependence, or geopolitical/war sensitivity.
21. `9. AI-related Analysis`
    Explain whether AI is a core product driver, infra customer, narrative tailwind, data/computing consumer, or mostly irrelevant. For chain+AI-fit requests, use [chain-ai-fit.md](chain-ai-fit.md) and rank the best AI directions instead of giving generic AI commentary.
22. `10. Latest Roadmap`
    Summarize the most recent roadmap, shipped items, near-term milestones, and what execution proof is still missing.
23. `11. Macroeconomic Impact`
    Explain how liquidity, rate cuts/hikes, dollar strength, Bitcoin dominance, ETF/fund flows, and broader risk appetite could affect the asset.
24. `12. Economic Policy Impact`
    Explain how regulation, enforcement posture, exchange listing policy, stablecoin legislation, securities treatment, taxation, and regional policy shifts could affect demand, accessibility, and valuation.
25. `13. 股市的影响`
    Explain how broader equity risk appetite, tech stock performance, sector proxies, ETF flows, listed-company exposure, or stock-market liquidity conditions could affect the asset. For stocks, explain factor exposure, style rotation, index flows, and peer-multiple transmission.
26. `14. 国际战争的影响`
    Explain how wars, sanctions, energy-price shocks, safe-haven behavior, cybersecurity escalation, capital controls, or payment fragmentation could affect the asset.
27. `15. 区块浏览器地址`
    Provide the main official or widely used explorer URL and explain what users can verify there, such as transactions, holders, contracts, validators, or governance activity. For stocks, replace this with investor-relations and filing links.
28. `16. 智能合约开发用什么语言`
    State the main smart contract language, execution environment, or VM stack. If smart contracts are not applicable, explain the actual developer stack instead. For stocks, this is usually `不适用` unless the company's technology stack is itself material to the business thesis.
29. `Key Risks`
    Include project-specific downside drivers.
30. `未证实但值得跟踪的问题`
    List unresolved debates, unverified claims, or open questions worth monitoring.
31. `Sources`
    Include markdown links for every meaningful source class you used.

## Depth Standard

- Prefer specifics over slogans.
- Include dates, metrics, and named counterparties when available.
- Mention disagreement when the market is split.
- Flag missing data instead of smoothing over it.
- Keep opinions tied to evidence.
- Explain second-order effects such as dilution pressure, incentive sustainability, liquidity reflexivity, or weak value capture.
- Explain whether upcoming unlocks are noise, manageable, or a real supply overhang.
- For stocks, translate the thesis into revenue growth, margin structure, cash flow conversion, balance-sheet quality, dilution, and valuation-multiple terms.
- Compare the asset with the most relevant competitors or substitutes when that changes the conclusion.
- Make each section answer both `what happened` and `why it matters`.
- In `历史大事件与影响意义`, choose events that changed the trajectory of the asset instead of filling space with ordinary milestones.
- On repeat coverage, make each section answer both `what changed since last time` and `why that change matters`.
- In `全方位多角度分析`, do not repeat earlier sections verbatim; synthesize them into a cross-angle judgment.
- A deep report should clearly distinguish between `bull case`, `bear case`, `priced-in consensus`, and `non-consensus opportunity or risk`.
- For chain-centric requests, `前景分析` should focus on chain competitiveness and `价值捕获分析` should separately explain whether the native token actually benefits.
- For chain-centric requests, do not claim decentralization, resilience, or security quality without node or validator evidence when such evidence should exist.
- For stock requests, do not use crypto-native metrics as filler; use equity metrics and clearly distinguish GAAP, non-GAAP, TTM, or forward numbers when relevant.
- For chain+AI-fit requests, the AI section should rank paths by fit, reject weak paths, and explain the implementation path.
- In `对标项目比较`, pick close peers and reach a judgment instead of listing peers mechanically.
- In `股市的影响`, explain transmission paths and relevant equity proxies instead of saying only that the asset is "positively correlated with stocks."
- In `国际战争的影响`, explain geopolitical transmission paths and sector-specific exposure instead of saying only that wars create "risk-off sentiment."
